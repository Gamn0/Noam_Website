About me

I was born on March 15, 1999, in Kfar Saba, Israel. There, I spent my early years and later moved to Zagreb, Croatia, at the age of 4. In Zagreb, I attended primary and secondary school (High School), completing my education in 2019. Subsequently, I applied and was accepted into the Faculty of Science at the University of Zagreb (PMF) for undergraduate studies in geophysics. Throughout my education, my interest expanded to include meteorology, seismology, and climatology. As my fascination grew, I recognized the importance of becoming proficient in programming to simulate, construct, and apply the concepts I was learning. Consequently, I decided to dedicate time to learning various programming and script languages. These skills not only contribute to my current self studies but also provide flexibility for potential future endeavors in the IT industry. I am exceptionally curious and persistent, especially when I feel my questions haven't been fully answered or logically debated. Additionally, I have a keen interest in politics and mental health, driven by ongoing questions that make me reflect on myself and the world. My hobbies include grappling sports such as Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and Judo, as well as Yoga and meditation. I am a peaceful individual who values human and animal rights, considering myself an animal rights activist, which led me to adopt veganism. As the world changes, so will I, but my curiosity will always endure.
